Former American Book Bindery Asbestos and Lead-
Based Paint Survey, Dormitory Authority,
State of New York


Matrix New World Engineering, Inc. (Matrix) prepared an asbestos and lead-based paint survey report for the former American Book Bindery Building, located at 406 West 31st Street in New York, New York. This survey and sampling was conducted in anticipation of the purchase and subsequent renovation of the building by the Dormitory Authority of the State of New York (DASNY) Fashion Institute of Technology (FIT). The property consists of a fifteen story structure, plus two subbasement levels known as the basement and the mezzanine. The building is approximately 250 feet in length and 100 feet in width at its widest point, and is constructed of brick and mortar exterior walls with concrete decks separating the floors. The loading dock consists of one floor at the mezzanine level and is attached to the main building off of the west wall.

The survey included an inspection of all accessible areas of the building. The building was inspected for friable materials and materials or products which are likely to contain asbestos. As part of the survey, MATRIX also collected information on the physical condition and location of suspect ACM in the potentially impacted portions of the building and characteristics of the building that may affect the likelihood of ACM being disturbed where fibers may be released and distributed. <

MATRIX collected bulk samples for determination of asbestos content by PLM analysis, Method EPA 600/R-93/116. Representative samples of the NOB samples that were negative for asbestos by PLM analysis were further analyzed by TEM. Samples were collected in general accordance with the procedures outlined in the AHERA regulations, 40 CFR Part 763.

A site survey and lead-based paint (LBP) inspection of the 406 West 31st Street property was conducted in general accordance with the Revised 1997 U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) Guidelines for the Evaluation and Control of Lead Based Paint Hazards in Housing. This consisted of a visual inspection of the various areas of the facility that would likely be impacted by the proposed modifications, as well as the performance of X-Ray Fluorescence (XRF) testing to detect the presence of lead in the painted surfaces.

The testing was accomplished in a manner representative of a cross section of various usage areas, components, and substrates in sections of the facility and exterior areas that may be impacted by the proposed facility renovation. The results of the XRF testing determined the presence or absence of lead in those various painted surfaces of the site.
